I was born in Kuantan, Pahang, in 2003. I am the eldest of 2 siblings. When I was born, my mother was only 20 years old, and my father was 24 years old. I grew up in Kuantan for 5 years. There, my father worked as a lorry driver, and my mother was a housewife. In 2010, we moved to my parents' village in Felda Keratong 4. After only one year there, we moved to Felda Padang Piol, Jerantut, because my father was taking care of his grandmother. I spent 8 years growing up there, where I made many good friends. In 2012, I finally got a little sister whom I had been eagerly waiting for. I was very happy. While growing up there, I learned many things, including the meaning of sacrifice because my mother worked in Kuala Lumpur for 2 years. At that time, I was 13 years old and took on the responsibility of caring for my younger sister, who was only 5 years old at the time. However, in 2015, my father's grandmother passed away. As a result, my parents decided to move back to Felda Keratong 4, where I had the opportunity to attend school for 2 years over there to sit for the SPM .

After completing SPM, I opted to pursue my studies at the STPM level. Initially, I received an offer for STPM at a school in Rompin, but I applied for a transfer to Temerloh. To this day, I have no regrets about choosing to move to SMK Abu Bakar, even though I didn't know anyone at the school initially. During my time striving through STPM, I met incredibly supportive and affectionate friends. The experiences with my friends are unforgettable. One of the most memorable moments was our frequent outings all the way to Kuala Lumpur. I still chuckle when I remember the mischief that got us kicked out of the dormitory. Hahaha! Moreover, I was deeply touched by how close-knit and helpful our classmates were in supporting each other academically. Undoubtedly, the time during Form Six was the most enriching experience of my youth.
